# BNOW.NET — Partner Strategy

Strategy doc (2026-07-07). Regional partner map, qualification rubric, and recommendation
on using existing influencer/intelligence-aligned contacts.

## 1. Core recommendation

Use the influencer-intelligence contacts, but do **not** lead with an influencer campaign.
Lead with a private expert-review/design-partner motion:

1. Ask each contact to pressure-test one region/module.
2. Ask for 2-3 introductions to buyers or analysts who would use the product operationally.
3. Turn the best reviewers into a visible "founding analyst circle" only after they have
   seen the evidence chain, scoreboard, and product workflow.

The product's trust claim is "clickable evidence + published validation." A public
endorsement before expert review weakens that claim. Expert introductions are more valuable
than reach until there is a weekly brief worth amplifying.

## 2. Partner roles

| Role | What they do | Best candidates | Risk to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Methodology validator** | Reviews source registry, reliability ratings, validation method | Ex-intel, think-tank, regional analysts | Generic pundit endorsement without product scrutiny |
| **Regional source scout** | Finds missing Telegram/X/RSS/local-language sources | Local OSINT researchers, journalists, regional analysts | Unvetted source lists that dilute reliability |
| **Design-buyer opener** | Introduces compliance, commodity, insurer, advisory buyers | Analysts with enterprise/government networks | Intros to commentators instead of buyers |
| **Channel/resale partner** | Uses BNOW as raw layer/API in their own advisory work | Political-risk consultancies, sanctions investigators | Custom consulting that distracts from product |
| **Data partner** | Supplies hard-to-get streams: AIS, corporate registries, sanctions, local data | Maritime, compliance, trade-data vendors | Expensive feeds before a buyer commits |

## 5. Qualification rubric

Score every candidate 1-5 before spending relationship capital:

| Criterion | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Buyer trust | Will a compliance lead, trader, insurer, or government analyst respect them? |
| Regional depth | Can they improve source coverage or interpretation for a specific theater? |
| Distribution quality | Do they reach buyers/operators, not just followers? |
| Methodology seriousness | Will they critique the evidence chain, not just like the concept? |
| Conflict/reputation risk | Are there public positions, client conflicts, or political baggage that could narrow the buyer base? |
| Commercial usefulness | Can they create intros, advisory value, channel resale, or data access within 30 days? |

Priority formula: **buyer trust + regional depth + commercial usefulness** beats raw audience
size. Audience only matters after product-market proof.

## 6. Outreach sequence

1. Prepare a 2-page evidence memo: scoreboard, source registry, claim-source traceability,
   one RU/UA digest, one Iran/Gulf example, one trade-evasion example.
2. Send a narrow ask: "Can you spend 30 minutes stress-testing whether this would be
   credible to [specific buyer segment]?"
3. In the call, ask for brutal gaps first, introductions second, endorsement never.
4. Offer three possible roles: unpaid reviewer, paid regional advisor, or channel/design
   partner introducer.
5. Convert only the serious reviewers into public-facing names after they have engaged
   with the product and are comfortable with the claims.
